International students at MU offer perspective on diverse issues during election year

The Urban Pioneer interviewed six international students from MU to gather their opinions about five divisive issues in the country: gun violence, immigration policy, the upcoming presidential election, racial tension, and LGBT rights.

Interviewees have spent between a year and three years in the U.S.. All but two of the students are undergraduates. Three interviewees are from China, while the rest come from India, Trinidad, and South Africa.
